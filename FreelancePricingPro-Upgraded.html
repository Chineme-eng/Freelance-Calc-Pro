Pricing tips
01
Do not price from fear
If you price just to avoid rejection, you buy yourself more work, more stress, and worse clients.
02
Cheap clients create hidden costs
Bad communication, extra revisions, slower approvals, and vague scope quietly destroy your profit if you do not price for them.
03
Packages sell better than raw hours
Clients understand outcomes better than time. Use your hourly math to protect yourself, then sell a clean package.
04
Retainers stabilize your income
A single recurring client can make your month feel much less fragile than chasing brand-new one-off projects.
05
Contract length deserves a discount
Reward commitment with a 5–10% retainer discount. You get stability; they get a better rate. Everyone wins.
06
Industry matters as much as skill
A copywriter for a law firm earns 3× more than one writing for a food blogger — same words, different budgets.
